Understanding 5G, Fibre, and LTE Connectivity

5G Connectivity

5G is the fifth generation of mobile network technology, offering significantly faster speeds, lower latency, and greater capacity compared to previous generations. It enables enhanced mobile broadband, supports massive IoT (Internet of Things) deployments, and provides ultra-reliable low-latency communications for applications like autonomous vehicles and remote surgery.

  • Speed: Up to 10 Gbps (theoretical maximum)
  • Latency: As low as 1 ms
  • Use Cases: Smart cities, AR/VR, IoT, high-definition streaming

Fibre Connectivity

Fibre (fiber optic) connectivity uses thin strands of glass or plastic to transmit data as pulses of light. It is known for its extremely high speeds, reliability, and resistance to electromagnetic interference. Fibre is ideal for both residential and business internet connections, supporting symmetrical upload and download speeds.

  • Speed: Up to 100 Gbps and beyond
  • Latency: Very low
  • Use Cases: Home broadband, business networks, data centers, backbone infrastructure

LTE Connectivity

LTE (Long Term Evolution) is a 4G wireless broadband technology widely used for mobile internet access. It offers faster speeds and lower latency than 3G, making it suitable for streaming, browsing, and mobile applications. LTE is available in most urban and rural areas, providing broad coverage.

  • Speed: Up to 300 Mbps (theoretical maximum)
  • Latency: Around 30-50 ms
  • Use Cases: Mobile phones, tablets, wireless hotspots, IoT devices
